Sitting on Concrete (No Bolting)

How it works

All three kits above are freestanding with base plates that sit flat on concrete. The structure's weight (400-600+ lbs for the Beaumont, heavy steel for the FORTRESS) provides stability. No drilling into the slab required.

Making it wind-safe without bolting

  • Add 50-100 lbs per post base: concrete pavers, weighted planters, or sandbags inside decorative covers.
  • Open-slat designs (all three kits) let wind pass through, which is the safest configuration.
  • If you add a canopy or shade sail later, it catches wind like a sail. Weight the bases more or consider bolting at that point.
  • The Beaumont at ~459 lbs is the heaviest and most naturally stable without anchoring.
  • If you ever want to bolt later, a hammer drill + Tapcon concrete screws is a 30-minute job.

Permits

A 12x12 is 144 sq ft, which exceeds the typical 120 sq ft exemption boundary. You will likely need a permit. Call your local building/zoning dept to confirm. 5-minute call saves thousands in fines.
  • Freestanding + open slat = simplest permit category
  • No electrical work needed (Beaumont's PowerPort is battery/solar, not hardwired)
  • Check setback requirements (distance from property line, typically 3-10 ft)
  • Permit fee if needed: ~$50-$150. Approval typically 1-3 weeks.

Assembly Tips

Before you start

  • Check your property survey. Know your property lines and setback requirements.
  • Measure the concrete pad. Make sure 12x12 fits with clearance for furniture (allow 3 ft around seating).
  • Have a helper. Beams are heavy and go up 8-10 ft.
  • Tools needed: drill/driver, socket set, level, step ladder, tape measure.

During assembly

  • Square your layout using the 3-4-5 triangle method. Measure diagonals (must be equal).
  • Level each post independently. Concrete pads can slope. Use shims under base plates if needed.
  • Pre-drill into cedar if using the Beaumont. Cedar splits easily near ends.
  • Brace posts temporarily with 2x4 diagonal braces while you get the beams up.
  • Tighten in stages. Hand-tight first, square everything, then final tighten.

After assembly

  • Add ballast to base plates (planters, pavers, sandbags) before the first windy day.
  • If cedar (Beaumont): inspect stain condition each spring, re-stain every 2-3 years.
  • If vinyl (Vita): power wash once a year to prevent mildew.
  • If steel (FORTRESS): just wipe down occasionally. That's it.
